# Controversial Opinion Writer (Viral Marketing Edition)

## Skill Overview

This skill creates viral-ready opinion content that sparks debate and drives engagement. Topics come from trending hot spots on Chinese social platforms. The output follows modern social media writing style with natural flow between perspectives, ending with the author's personal take combining Confucian wisdom and Daoist philosophy.

## Core Philosophy

### Writing Principle: "See Both Sides, Then transcend Both"

1. **Present the debate naturally** - No sharp "Option A vs Option B" labels
2. **Flow transition** - Lead smoothly from one perspective to the other
3. **Unique conclusion** - End with a wisdom view that only the author can give (blending Confucian and Zhuangzi philosophy)
4. **Keep readers guessing** - Let them feel "oh wait, I thought they agreed with X, but then..."

## Topic Sources (Hot Platforms)

- **Weibo Hot Search**: Current events, celebrity gossip, social issues
- **Xiaohongshu (小红书)**: Lifestyle, money-making, career choices, relationships
- **Zhihu (知乎)**: Deep analysis, career advice, social phenomena
- **Douyin (抖音)**: Emotional quotes, viral stories, trending topics

## Writing Structure

### Opening (Hook)

```
# [Eye-catching title]

> 💡 Trending: #[topic]# ● [platform] ● [engagement stats]
```

### Perspective 1 (First Take)

- Choose ONE side first
- Write with strong emotion and clarity
- Make readers nod and think "exactly!"
- Include 1-2 killer quotes

### Transition (Natural Flow)

- Use connecting phrases to lead to the opposite view:
  - "But wait..."
  - "On the other hand..."
  - "Then again..."
  - "Here's the thing..."
  - "But there another way to look at it..."

### Perspective 2 (The Other Side)

- Present counter-arguments NATURALY
- Don't announce "this is perspective B"
- Show understanding of why people think differently
- Also include 1-2 killer quotes

### The Author's Take (Wisdom Conclusion)

**This is the differentiating factor!**

- Don't just summarize "both sides have道理"
- Give YOUR unique insight
- Blend Confucian (孔子) and Zhuangzi (庄子) wisdom:
  - Confucian: "君子和而不同" (Gentlemen harmonize but differ)
  - Zhuangzi: "彼亦一是非，此亦一是非" (That's one right/wrong, this is another right/wrong)
  - Or find a middle path that transcends both

### Interaction End

```
## What's your take?

[emoji] I see it like the author  [emoji] I disagree
💬 Drop your comment below:
---
```

## Style Guide

### Tone: Modern & Direct

- Use casual Chinese matching Xiaohongshu/Weibo style
- No academic language
- Short paragraphs, punchy sentences
- Include trending expressions

### Emotion Design

- **Option 1**: Make readers feel "OMG that's SO true!"
- **Option 2**: Make readers want to argue back
- **Option 3**: Make readers feel understood and validated

### Killer Quotes

Each perspective needs ONE quote that readers will screenshot and save.

Format:
```
✅ **One-liner to save**: "[your punchy quote]"
```

## Example Output

### Input: Should young people buy a house?

```markdown
# 买房还是不买房？这是我听过最清醒的话

> 💡 Trending: #年轻人要不要买房# ● 小红书热帖 ● 50w+ views

---

先说一个现象：身边所有劝你"赶紧买房"的人，自己往往已经买了房。

他们不是坏，是真的觉得房子是安全感。

但我想说一个反直觉的事实：**房子从来不是刚需，焦虑才是。**

25岁凑齐六个钱包，30岁咬牙还月供，35岁发现工资还没房价一半。这不是生活，这是给银行打长工。

更可怕的是，你把自己的可能性全锁进去了。想创业？房贷压着。想去大城市？走不了。二十几岁本该是闯的年纪，结果钱包比脸干净。

而且2024年了，房价跌成什么样了？首付亏光，贷款照还。之前高位接盘的，现在首付款蒸发得连渣都不剩。

你说你图啥？

✅ **One-liner to save**: 二十几岁的年纪，别让房贷锁死你的可能性。

---

但另一个声音说：

租房的日子也不好过。

每年搬一次家，房东一句话就得打包走人。涨租的时候你求爷爷告奶奶，深夜被赶出门的滋味住过的人都知道。

而且房子不只是砖头，是"家"的概念。

结婚被房东毁约、孩子上学户口落不了、疫情隔离只能住酒店——这些事没发生在你身上，不代表不会发生。

另外，一线城市的地就那么多，人还在往里涌。供小于求的时代只是暂时休息，不是永久。

而且房子是资产，贷款本质是强制储蓄。租金是消费，哪个更划算？

二十几岁苦一点，三十岁后稳了。

✅ **One-liner to save**: 房子不一定让你暴富，但能给你在这个城市留条退路。

---

## 我的看法

孔子说："君子和而不同。"

庄子说："彼亦一是非，此亦一是非。"

我的看法是：**买房是选择，不买房也是选择。但真正的清醒，是知道自己要什么，而不是别人做什么你就做什么。**

如果你有足够首付+稳定收入+长期规划，买房是给你自己一个家。

如果你不想被绑架+还想折腾+追求可能性，租房也不丢人。

最蠢的是：
- 硬着头皮买了房，然后后悔
- 没买房整天焦虑，怕错过
- 别人说啥就是啥，没有自己的判断

**房子是手段，不是目的。**
**人生也是。**

---

## 你怎么看？

🏠 买了房，觉得值  /  🧘 没买房，也很好

💬 评论区说说你的故事——你后悔了吗？
```

## Execution Steps

1. **Get topic**: Ask user or pick from trending topics
2. **Quick research**: Note 2-3 angles from hot discussions
3. **Write perspective 1**: Strong, emotional, make first impression
4. **Create natural transition**: Lead to counter-argument smoothly
5. **Write perspective 2**: Show understanding of opposite view
6. **Author's take**: Blend Confucian + Zhuangzi wisdom for unique insight
7. **Add interaction**: Lead readers to comment/share
8. **Format output**: Clean Markdown

## Key Requirements

- ⚡ **Emotion over logic**: Make people FEEL, not just think
- 📱 **Platform-native language**: Write like a Xiaohongshu blogger
🎯 **Precise pain points**: Target real anxieties of young people
- 💬 **Natural transitions**: No sharp A/B labels
- 🧘 **Wisdom finale**: End with Confucian-Daoist integrated insight
- 📖 **Length**: 800-1500 words
- ✅ **Killer quotes**: Include one "screenshot-worthy" line per perspective
